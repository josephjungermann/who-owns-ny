Who Owns Their Home NYC

By Diana DuCroz


New York City stands out from other American cities in that most residents are renters, not owners. The high cost of housing makes homeownership prohibitive for the average person, but for those who an afford it, ownership provides many benefits - stability and permanence, protection from unscrupulous landlords, the freedom to renovate to one's taste among them. Homeownership is also the top way for middle-class Americans to build wealth in the form of equity.

Historically, people of color have been shut out of the homeownership market through a variety of ways - legal prohibitions against living in 'white' neighborhoods, bank redlining, the lack of generational wealth to pass down. Even today when segregation and redlining are officially illegal, a racial disparity still exists. Whites have the highest rate of homeownership in New York City, followed by Asians householders.
